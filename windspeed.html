Wind Speed vs. Latitude

This visualization shows the relationship between a city's wind speed and its latitude on July 11th, 2019. The cities' latitudes are plotted along the x axis, and the highest wind speed in mph is plotted along the y axis. Data points are concentrated along the bottom half of the plot, with the vast majority accounting for wind speeds below 10 mph.

This visualization reveals that globally, wind speeds tend to not exceed 10 mph, and in many cases, stays below 5 mph. Wind speed of above 20 mph were very rare and mostly occurred in the lowest altitudes (more than 30 degrees south of the equator). Moderately high wind speeds (between 10 and 15 mph) appeared to be more common the farther north a city was located, though that may have been a result of the fact that there were more cities found in the northern hemisphere than in the southern hemisphere. Generally speaking, wind speeds seemed to be more consistent at latitudes that were farther away from the equator.
